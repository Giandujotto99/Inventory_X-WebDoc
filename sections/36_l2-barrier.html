Revision Barrier

The Revision Barrier is the fundamental synchronization mechanism of Layer 2 of Inventory_X, designed to ensure that a cross-inventory transaction is considered final by the client only when both involved inventories have actually received and applied the server’s authoritative changes.

Here are the technical and logical details of how it works:

1. Definition and Purpose

The barrier represents the minimum authoritative revision that the client must observe on both endpoints (Primary and Secondary) to close the reconciliation process of an operation.

In a multiplayer context, data from two different inventory components may arrive at the client in separate network packets and at different times.

Without the barrier, the client could “confirm” an action based only on the server’s response (ack), risking displaying a partial or inconsistent state if one of the two inventories has not yet been updated locally by replication.

2. Server-Side Generation

The barrier is generated exclusively by the server at the end of a successful atomic commit.

The server does not “estimate” the barrier in advance; after applying the mutations, it samples the real authoritative revisions of the two involved components through the Layer 1 bridge.

These two values (one for the Primary and one for the Secondary) are inserted into the FInventoryInteractionRevisionBarrier structure and sent to the client within the operation ack.

3. Layer 1 Support

To make the barrier operational, the system requires an extension to Layer 1: each UInventoryComponent must expose and replicate an authoritative component-level revision (ReplicatedAuthoritativeRevision).

This revision is incremented at every commit that modifies the inventory state and is replicated to all interested clients.

The identity of the authoritative component remains the only source of truth for verifying that the barrier has been reached.

4. Satisfaction and Reconciliation (Client-Side)

On the owner client, the barrier governs the lifecycle of prediction:

Waiting queue: When the client receives a success ack, the operation is not confirmed immediately. Instead, it is placed into a “barrier waiting” queue (PendingAuthoritativeAcks).

Continuous verification: The system constantly compares the barrier values with the current revisions of the local components. The barrier is satisfied only when both endpoints have a revision greater than or equal to the required one.

Reconcile trigger: Only once the barrier is satisfied does the prediction manager start the reconciliation protocol (rollback, remap, and replay), ensuring that the local authoritative base is complete and consistent.

5. Implementation Characteristics

Blueprint Visibility: By design philosophy, the barrier and raw revisions are treated as infrastructural details and are not exposed directly to Blueprint logic as manipulable numbers, instead preferring semantic predicates and updated views.

Serialization: The barrier supports NetSerialize to optimize network transport, ensuring that synchronization data weighs as little as possible on bandwidth.
