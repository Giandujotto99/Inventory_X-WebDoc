Policy Definitions

The UInventoryInteractionPolicyDefinition asset constitutes the normative "brain" of interactions in Layer 2. It is a UDataAsset that allows you to define, in an entirely data-driven way, who can interact with a given chest or storage and what they are authorized to do.

While the Provider abstraction identifies "where" and "which" external inventory is, the Policy establishes the rules of engagement.

1. Capability Management (Capability Mask)

Unlike Layer 1, where rules are often intrinsic to the object, Layer 2 uses an explicit capability model. The Policy defines three distinct permission masks based on the session state:

GrantedReadCapabilities: Permissions granted when the session is in read-only mode (e.g., ViewExternalInventory, UpgradeToWriteLock).

GrantedWriteCapabilities: Additional permissions unlocked only when the player holds the Writer Lock (e.g., Deposit, Withdraw, Split, Merge, Swap).

GrantedSuspendedCapabilities: The very few actions allowed while the session is suspended (typically only ResumeSuspendedSession).

2. Identity Filters (Tag Queries)

The Policy does not trust client declarations, but evaluates two types of queries based on GameplayTags to authorize the opening of the link:

Requester Query: Checks the tags of the player attempting access (e.g., Faction, Team, or possession of a logical "key").

Primary Owner Query: Checks the tags of the player’s own inventory (e.g., to allow interaction only if the player has equipped a specific backpack or hacking kit).

3. Reachability and Lifecycle (Range & Visibility)

The Policy establishes the physical and logical boundaries of the interaction, preventing the system from remaining active under unsafe conditions:

Range Configuration: Defines the maximum interaction distance and whether line of sight is required (Line of Sight).

Suspension Management: Determines whether temporary loss of range or visibility should cause the immediate closure of the session or its Suspension with a "grace window" (Grace Window) for restoration.

4. "Fail-Closed" Philosophy

To ensure maximum security, the architecture adopts a Fail-Closed approach: if a provider does not have a Policy assigned or if the Policy is configured inconsistently, the system automatically denies any opening attempt. There are no implicit permissive fallbacks; every right must be explicitly granted by data.

5. Editor Validation

The asset includes internal checks to prevent absurd configurations (for example, a policy that requires a range but sets the distance to zero), reporting errors or warnings to the designer even before saving.
