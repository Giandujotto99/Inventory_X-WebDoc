Vision and Use Cases

Layer 2 of Inventory_X is the layer designed to manage the exchange of items between two distinct inventories (for example, between the player and a chest or a corpse in the game world).

While Layer 1 handles the internal logic of a single component, Layer 2 introduces the rules to coordinate actions involving two different owners.

Vision and Purpose: Player <-> World Interaction

The main goal is to provide a solid architecture to manage any type of external inventory (chests, storage, backpacks on the ground) in a secure and synchronized way between players. The pillars of this system are:

Session of Interaction: Every time a player interacts with a container in the world, the server creates an authorized "session" to manage that specific link.

Capability-Based Model: Instead of generic permissions, the system uses specific capabilities granted by the server, such as "can view", "can take", or "can deposit".

Multi-View / Single-Writer Access: Many players can look at the same container at the same time, but only one at a time can obtain the "write permission" (lock) to move or modify the items inside it.

Atomic Transfers: Operations between two inventories follow the "all or nothing" rule: an item cannot disappear from one inventory without correctly appearing in the other, preventing data loss or duplication.

Practical Use Cases

The system is built to handle the typical flows of modern games:

Deposit and Withdrawal: Classic management through drag-and-drop between the player panel and the external container panel.

Stack Operations Between Endpoints: Ability to split or merge stacks of items while they are being moved from one inventory to another.

Nested Item Movement: Full support for transferring bags or backpacks that themselves contain other items; the entire hierarchy is preserved during the transfer between the two components.

Multiplayer Synchronization: Ensures that if a player (who has write permission) moves an item into a chest, all other players who are looking at it see the change instantly through Layer 1.

Different Inventory Providers: Support for interactions with different entities (bodies, vendors, stash) through a single interface that regulates security and interaction distance.
